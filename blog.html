H-1B Cap: Registration Timeline and Wage-Weighted Selection

USCIS has officially confirmed the initial registration period for the FY 2027 H-1B cap will run from March 4 to March 19, 2026. This season solidifies the implementation of the wage-weighted selection process intended to prioritize higher-skilled workers over entry-level roles.

USCIS has officially confirmed the initial registration period for the FY 2027 H-1B cap will run from March 4 to March 19, 2026. This season solidifies the implementation of the wage-weighted selection process intended to prioritize higher-skilled workers over entry-level roles.

What this means for applicants: The new rule heavily favors beneficiaries whose offered wage equals or exceeds the Level 4 prevailing wage for their occupational classification. International students graduating and transitioning to H-1B via OPT may face significantly lower selection probabilities unless securing top-tier compensation packages.

Employers are advised to review Labor Condition Application (LCA) data thoroughly and align salary structures prior to the March 4th registration opening to remain compliant and competitive.

Disparities in US Tourist Visa Scheduling Times

Data reveals massive gaps in wait times across consulates. Chennai currently offers appointments in 1 month, while Mumbai trails at 10 months.

Recent data releases indicate massive geographical gaps in wait times across US consulates in India. For example, Chennai currently offers B1/B2 appointments in roughly 1 month, while Mumbai severely trails at nearly 10 months.

Applicants for B1/B2 visas (business/tourist) should strategically consider applying at consulates with lighter caseloads if their travel is time-sensitive. However, applicants must keep in mind that language-specific interviews (e.g., Telugu, Marathi) might be restricted to particular regional locations.
